Key Principles

Production access is restricted. CTO and DevOps only. Break-glass procedures for emergency access. All production access is logged and reviewed. Non-technical team members retain prototype access only. This is a SOC 2 requirement, not a preference.

The AI pipeline is a force multiplier, not a headcount replacement. The Scout/Smith/Anvil pipeline handles approximately 90% of feature development. Human engineers own financial logic correctness, security response, integration maintenance, and production incidents — the 10% that carries regulatory liability.

Integration count drives headcount. One mid-level engineer can own two to three active integrations. Each integration is a maintenance commitment, a compliance obligation, and a failure domain. Integration growth beyond five triggers a second mid-level hire.

Architecture is owned by the CTO + DevOps pairing at this stage. A dedicated architect role is not warranted until ANVL has three or more product teams building in parallel, or until Phase 2 hardware development begins.
